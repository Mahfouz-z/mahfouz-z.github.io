Mohamed M. Shahawy is a computer science PhD candidate at EPFL, supervised by Prof. Paolo Ienne in the Processor Architecture Lab. His research centers on building a complete, open-source hardware–software stack for task-level parallel computing on FPGAs, spanning architecture design, multi-FPGA scaling, and compiler automation. His work addresses the efficient execution of irregular, control-dominated algorithms—such as graph analytics and recursive computations—that today run almost exclusively on general-purpose CPUs, with few energy-efficient or specialized acceleration alternatives available. He has published at IEEE FCCM, ACM FPGA, and IEEE FPL, and received a Best Paper Award nomination at FCCM 2024. He holds a BSc in computer engineering with highest honors from the American University in Cairo.
